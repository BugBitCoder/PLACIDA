# 🧪 Placida — User Testing Script (Week 4)

## 📝 Introduction for the Tester
"Thank you for participating in our usability test for Placida. Placida is a mental wellness companion designed to help you track your mood and manage stress privately. Today, we'll ask you to perform a few tasks. Please 'think out loud' as you navigate, sharing what you notice, what confuses you, or what you enjoy. Remember, we are testing the app, not you—there are no wrong answers."

---

## 🏗️ Tasks

### Task 1: Mood Logging
**Action**: Log your current mood. Use the emoji picker, add a short note about your day, and save it.
- **Observation**: Did the user find the emoji picker immediately? Was the 'Save' feedback clear?
- **Success Criteria**: A new entry appears in the Dashboard history.

### Task 2: Dashboard Exploration
**Action**: Navigate to the Dashboard and describe the information you see.
- **Observation**: Do they understand what the "Streak" represents? Did they notice the mood trend chart?
- **Success Criteria**: User can correctly explain at least 3 displayed statistics.

### Task 3: Stress Management
**Action**: Start a breathing session and complete at least 3 full cycles.
- **Observation**: Is the animation timing intuitive? Do they know when a cycle starts/ends?
- **Success Criteria**: The 'Cycles' counter in the UI reaches 3.

### Task 4: Conversational Support
**Action**: Open the Chatbot and have a 5-message conversation about something bothering you.
- **Observation**: Do the bot's replies feel natural? Did they notice the quick-reply chips?
- **Success Criteria**: User completes the interaction without reported confusion or frustration.

### Task 5: Weekly Reflection
**Action**: Find your weekly mood summary and look for a journaling prompt.
- **Observation**: Can the user navigate to the 'Summary' page without external guidance?
- **Success Criteria**: User reaches `summary.html` successfully.

---

## ❓ Post-Test Questions
1. What was the most useful feature you encountered?
2. If you could change one thing about the navigation, what would it be?
3. How did the 'private/local-only' nature of the app influence your willingness to share your feelings?
